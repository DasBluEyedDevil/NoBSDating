# NoBS Dating - Beta Communication Plan

## Overview

This document outlines the communication strategy for the NoBS Dating beta program, including email templates, update schedules, and response guidelines to ensure clear, consistent, and timely communication with beta testers.

## Communication Channels

### Primary Channels
1. **Email**: beta@nobsdating.app - Main communication channel
2. **Discord/Slack**: Beta tester community for real-time discussion
3. **In-App Notifications**: Critical updates and announcements
4. **GitHub Issues**: Technical discussions and bug tracking

### Communication Frequency
- **Welcome Email**: Day 0 (immediately upon acceptance)
- **Weekly Updates**: Every Monday at 10 AM
- **Critical Updates**: As needed (security issues, major bugs, service disruptions)
- **Phase Transition**: End of each beta phase
- **Surveys**: Mid-phase and end-of-phase

## Response Time Commitments

### Email Response Times
- **Critical bugs (P0)**: Acknowledge within 4 hours (during business hours)
- **High priority (P1)**: Acknowledge within 8 hours
- **General feedback**: Acknowledge within 24 hours
- **Feature requests**: Acknowledge within 48 hours
- **Questions**: Respond within 12 hours (business days)

### Weekend/After-Hours
- P0 bugs: Monitored 24/7, best effort response
- Other inquiries: Response by next business day

## Communication Guidelines

### Tone and Voice

**Do:**
- Be transparent and honest
- Acknowledge mistakes quickly
- Use clear, simple language
- Show appreciation frequently
- Be personal and human
- Celebrate wins together

**Don't:**
- Use jargon without explanation
- Make promises you can't keep
- Be defensive about criticism
- Ignore negative feedback
- Over-promise timelines

### Transparency Principles

1. **Be Honest About Issues**: If something is broken, say so clearly
2. **Share Timelines Realistically**: Under-promise and over-deliver
3. **Admit When You Don't Know**: "We're investigating" is better than speculation
4. **Show the Process**: Share what you're working on and why
5. **Credit Testers**: Acknowledge contributions publicly (with permission)

### Handling Negative Feedback

1. **Thank the Tester**: Appreciate their honesty
2. **Acknowledge the Issue**: Show you understand their frustration
3. **Explain Context** (if appropriate): Share constraints or reasoning
4. **Share Next Steps**: What will you do about it?
5. **Follow Up**: Close the loop when addressed

**Example Response:**
```
Hi [Name],

Thank you for the candid feedback about [issue]. You're absolutely right that [acknowledge the problem].

We understand this is frustrating because [show empathy]. Here's what's happening:

[Context or explanation]

We're prioritizing this as [priority level] and plan to [action] by [timeline].

I'll follow up with you once we've made progress.

Thanks for helping us improve.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Escalation Procedures

### When to Escalate to Leadership

- Security vulnerabilities discovered
- Widespread critical bug affecting >50% of users
- Negative press or public controversy
- Legal concerns raised by testers
- Repeated failures to meet commitments

### Escalation Process

1. Email team lead immediately
2. Document the issue and impact
3. Prepare summary and recommendation
4. Schedule emergency call if needed
5. Draft communication plan
6. Approve messaging before sending
